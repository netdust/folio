`scripts/check-invariants.ts` + `pre-commit-invariants.sh` are done and green

- Note: the hook checks the invariants DOC hasn't drifted off its code anchors (file/symbol/
  line citations) — it does NOT verify the properties hold (that's the `invariant-auditor`).

### Option C — Operator confirm-gate UX polish (the cockpit's rough edge)
The confirm gate now pauses cleanly, but two UX gaps remain (both in
`@tasks/retro-follow-ups.md`, both real, both observed in the real-BYOK smoke):
- **Cockpit does not auto-resume an in-progress conversation on reload.** A user paused
  on a confirm card who reloads the page lands on a blank "How can the operator help?" and
  can't get back to click the card. `cockpit-chat.tsx` supports `conversationId` resume but
  doesn't auto-load the most-recent active conversation on mount.
- (Lower) the confirm card's framing / re-entry after approval could use a once-over now
  that the fail-the-run bug is gone.

### Option D — Operator identity architectural cleanup (highest-leverage debt)
From the max-effort `/code-review` on the operator fixes (findings #7, #8 in
`@tasks/retro-follow-ups.md`). The operator's synthetic id (`operator:_operator`) lives in
an FK-shaped `token.agentId`, forcing every consumer to special-case OR null it (already 2
remediations in 2 files). And `eventActor` is an optional-with-human-default param threaded
through 3 services + 6 sites — a future write service that forgets it silently disables the
autonomy gate for that path, no compile error.
- **The fix collapses both:** decide the operator's token identity in ONE place (null the
  agentId everywhere + an explicit operator marker, OR resolve the sentinel once at
  mint/loadContext), and replace the optional `eventActor` with a single actor object
  carrying both `fkId` and `eventIdentity`, derived once at the registry boundary.
- Do this **before a 4th `agentId` consumer or write service inherits the trap.** It's the
  kind of thing that's cheap now and expensive after it spreads.

**My recommendation:** if you want a quick win + to close the views thread, do **A(b) + B**
together (both small, both finish started work). If you'd rather harden the platform, **D**
is the highest-leverage and the riskiest-to-defer.
